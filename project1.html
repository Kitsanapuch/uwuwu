Guava Quality Analysis

Presented at the Siam Physics Congress (SPC2025), held from June 11–13, 2025, at Pullman Hotel, Khon Kaen, organized by Khon Kaen University. This research, conducted in the field of Biophysics, was showcased via a poster presentation titled 'Applications of Electrical Properties in the Postharvest Quality Assessment of Guava Fruit.'

Key features include:

  • Non-Destructive Quality Evaluation
  • Analysis of Electrical Properties
  • Poster Presentation at SPC2025
  • Data Visualization and Interpretation

Instrument Utilized

LCR Meter

Operational Methodology

In recent decades, numerous studies have been conducted to develop accurate and precise food quality evaluation techniques. Increasing consumer demand for information about internal fruit quality, such as freshness and sweetness, necessitates cost-effective detection methods. The need for information on postharvest fruit quality makes it possible to utilize electrical properties as a novel approach for quality evaluation. In this study, the electrical parameters—resistance, conductance, and impedance—of harvested guava fruits were measured using a high-precision LCR (inductance, capacitance, and resistance) meter. All electrical property measurements were performed at a frequency of 1 kHz. Sugar content, measured in Brix, was also determined. The relationship between electrical resistance and sugar content was investigated during a 21-day storage period. Results showed electrical resistance and impedance decreased with increasing storage time, while electrical conductance and sugar content increased. The loss of fruit moisture during storage may explain the decrease in electrical resistance and the increase in electrical capacitance and sugar content.

Outcomes Achieved

Received guidance from expert academics, acquired knowledge on advancing the project for agricultural applications, and developed skills in presenting scientific research.
